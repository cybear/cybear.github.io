If you're into CSS, chances are that you already know most
of these brilliant people. I am really happy to have learned from
talks, blog posts and tweets by them. So without further ado, here
are my top 5 most inspiring women in CSS:

Nicole Sullivan

blogged and talked about Object-Oriented CSS
(OOCSS) in 2009 (or earlier?) which was possibly the first attempts
at articulating solutions for some of the biggest problems with
CSS. (Nowadays I'm really excited about SMACSS by Jonathan Snook.)
On her site Stubbornella I also first learned about CSS performance
and CSS bad practices.

Lea Verou

, CSS Pioneer

Divya Manian

Ana Tudor

Meagan Fisher
